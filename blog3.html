Hackathons are more than just competitions; they're launchpads for innovation, collaboration, and personal growth. They offer developers a chance to push their boundaries, build amazing projects, and connect with a vibrant community. And for some, hackathons are truly life-changing.

Winning the AfroTech AI hackathon catapulted Increase on an exciting journey to the United States. Discover how Increase navigated this incredible journey, and how his hackathon experience opened doors to a world of possibilities.

üöÄ The Spark of Interest

Increase's passion for computers started early. He'd spend holidays at his dad's office teaching himself graphic design with CorelDraw. However, after losing all his work from the computers one day, he took a break from technology for years.

Fast forward a decade, and his love for tech was rekindled through music and video production. He dove into tools like Reason, Adobe Premiere, and Audacity, even volunteering as a media director for a non-profit. This creative outlet reignited his interest in building with technology.

From there, he transitioned to software development, starting with low-code platforms like GoodBarber and WordPress, then experimenting with PHP, PhoneGap, Java, and Adobe Air. As the industry evolved, so did he, learning React, JavaScript, Python, Flutter, and now, AI.

"When I saw the health track, I knew it was my chance to build Anecdotal AI, a health tech app I'd always dreamed of creating."

üí° Advice for Aspiring Developers

  • Embrace Continuous Learning The "stack" in full-stack development keeps growing. As tools and technologies advance, so do the expectations placed on developers. Staying adaptable and committed to improving your skills is key.
  • Learn from Failure Increase didn't win his first Devpost hackathon, but that experience taught him a lot. After reviewing winning projects and gathering feedback, he refined his skills and approach.
  • Collaborate and Team Up Find teammates with complementary skills on platforms like Devpost. For his second win, he teamed up with a talented UX designer. As he says: "If you want to go fast, go alone; but if you want to go far, you need a team."
  • Always Put in Your Best Consistency and dedication are crucial. Every hackathon is an opportunity to learn, grow, and potentially change your life.
